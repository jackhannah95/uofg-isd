Bios

Jack

Graduated from the University of Glasgow with a BSc (Hons) in Statistics in 2017. Joined ISD as an Information Analyst in July 2017. Works predominantly on using R to automate the creation of statistical reports and modernise the way in which they are presented.

David

Graduated from the University of Glasgow with an M.Sci in Statistics in 2015. Did a placement year at Santander from 2013-14. Joined ISD as an Information Analyst in July 2015. Works on publishing data on hospital mortality and transforming how ISD releases its data more generally.

David’s work

Two main projects:

  • Hospital Standardised Mortality Ratios
    • Using logistic regression to predict the number of deaths to occur within acute hospitals across Scotland
  • Reproducible Analytical Pipeline (RAP)
    • Using R and RMarkdown to automate a publication process start to finish

Jack’s work

Some of the more interesting projects:

  • Long Term Conditions
    • Using poisson regression to predict prevalence rates in Lanarkshire of various long term conditions, such as liver disease and arthritis
  • Shiny
    • Transforming traditional PDF statistical publications into interactive dashboards using the shiny package in R
  • Open source contributions
    • Making contributions to the testthat and tidylog R packages via GitHub

Other interesting work in ISD

  • Mortality trends
    • Investigating how trends in mortality are influenced by government austerity, pressure on NHS services and influenza outbreaks
  • Suicide statistics
    • Gets a lot of media attention (e.g news articles about latest figures from the BBC, Herald and Scotsman)
